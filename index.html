Lenovo's Superfish Vulnerability

If you are using a Lenovo PC and running the pre-installed Windows, chances are that you might be infected with the Superfish adware. This adware injects third-party ads on google searches and other websites.

The way Superfish accomplishes this, leaves you vulnerable to the Man in the Middle (MITM) attack, even on secure websites (https://) like Google and Facebook.

FAQs

How do I check if I am vulnerable?

To check if your PC is infected by Superfish, you should visit https://filippo.io/Badfish/ in all your browsers (IE, Chrome and Firefox). This site also contains steps to remove Superfish, if you are effected.

How do I remove Superfish?

Please follow these steps. Lenovo has also provided an official uninstallation guide. Make sure you visit https://filippo.io/Badfish/ in all your browsers (IE, Chrome and Firefox) after uninstalling to confirm that you are not vulnerable.

Which Lenovo models are effected?

As per Lenovo's official statement, these models are effected:

  • G Series: G410, G510, G710, G40-70, G50-70, G40-30, G50-30, G40-45, G50-45
  • U Series: U330P, U430P, U330Touch, U430Touch, U530Touch
  • Y Series: Y430P, Y40-70, Y50-70
  • Z Series: Z40-75, Z50-75, Z40-70, Z50-70
  • S Series: S310, S410, S40-70, S415, S415Touch, S20-30, S20-30Touch
  • Flex Series: Flex2 14D, Flex2 15D, Flex2 14, Flex2 15, Flex2 14(BTM), Flex2 15(BTM), Flex 10
  • MIIX Series: MIIX2-8, MIIX2-10, MIIX2-11
  • YOGA Series: YOGA2Pro-13, YOGA2-13, YOGA2-11BTM, YOGA2-11HSW
  • E Series: E10-30

I use my own copy of Windows/other operating system, but my machine is in the above list. Can I still be vulnerable?

No, most probably you are not effected by Superfish. You should still confirm from here.

I never use Internet Explorer, should I still be worried?

Superfish effects all browsers (IE, Chrome, Firefox among others).
